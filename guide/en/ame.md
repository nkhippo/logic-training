# Sky-Rain-Umbrella Tab

From business data or market information, you work through **Sky (facts) → Rain (interpretation) → Umbrella (action)**.

- **Sky (facts)** — Numbers and events you can verify objectively
- **Rain (interpretation)** — What you logically infer from facts
- **Umbrella (action)** — What to do next based on that interpretation

The goal is to **not mix facts and interpretation** and to keep logical links explicit.

## Skills You Build

- Separating facts from views and opinions
- Drawing reasonable interpretations from facts
- Proposing actions grounded in interpretation
- Seeing that other interpretations are possible

## Question Types

| Type | Content | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| Sort facts (Sky) | Separate facts from interpretation / opinion | 2–5 |
| Interpretation (Rain) | Write a logical reading from facts | All |
| Next action (Umbrella) | Propose action from the reading | All |
| Basis of reasoning | Which facts / rules led to your reading | 4–5 |
| Self-check | Ask whether your reading is really justified | 4–5 |
| When the rule fails | Cases where a given rule does not apply (deductive) | 4–5, deductive |

From difficulty 3, “sort facts” uses **two fields**: parts stated as facts vs parts stated as interpretation/opinion. Difficulty 2 uses one field (facts-only article).

## Themes and Difficulty

| Difficulty | Theme area | Problem type | Questions |
|---|---|---|:---:|
| 1 (Intro) | Sales / marketing | Facts only | 2 |
| 2 (Basic) | HR / org, project management | Facts only | 3 |
| 3 (Standard) | IT / systems, marketing | Mixed facts and interpretation | 3 |
| 4 (Advanced) | Strategy, sales | Mixed | 5 |
| 5 (Expert) | Strategy, IT / systems | Mixed | 5 |

Difficulty 4–5 may include **deductive** items with a stated rule or premise. Combine rule and facts to derive your reading.

## Feedback

Two axes:

- **Logical chain** — Any leap from facts → rain → umbrella?
- **Alternative reading** — Another interpretation from the same facts, for comparison

## Tips

When writing Rain, check which fact in the article supports it. If the basis is not in the text, it is assumption, not interpretation.

Umbrella must follow logically from Rain. Can you explain in one sentence why that action follows?
