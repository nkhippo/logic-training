# Summarize Tab

You read an AI-generated business document (minutes, report, proposal, etc.) and answer prompts within a character limit.
The goal is to **keep claims and evidence from the text only** and cut concrete detail.

## Skills You Build

- Turning specifics into abstract claims
- Not importing outside knowledge or personal opinion
- Choosing what to keep under a tight limit

## Question Types

| Type | Content | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| Main claim | Summarize the document’s main claim within the limit | All |
| Term / expression | Explain a business term or phrase in the text | 2–5 |
| Reason | Explain why the author makes that claim | 3–5 |

## Material and Difficulty

| Difficulty | Document type | Length | Questions |
|---|---|:---:|:---:|
| 1 (Basic) | Internal email, notices | 300 chars | 1 |
| 2 (Standard) | Minutes, progress report | 400 chars | 2 |
| 3 (Applied) | Proposal / plan excerpt | 500 chars | 3 |
| 4 (Advanced) | Analysis / survey report | 500–2000 chars | 3 |
| 5 (Expert) | Strategy / consulting document | 500–4000 chars | 3 |

## How Feedback Is Scored

Four axes:

- **Accuracy** — Did you capture claims and evidence correctly?
- **Conciseness** — Within the character limit?
- **Expression** — Clear paraphrase in your own words?
- **Selection** — Kept claims and evidence, cut examples appropriately?

## Tips

While reading, separate **main claim**, **evidence**, and **examples**.
In a summary, drop examples and keep claim + evidence. Do not add facts or opinions that are not in the document.
