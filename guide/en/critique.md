# Critical Reading Tab

You find logical weaknesses in business writing (proposals, approval docs, analysis reports) and answer in your own words.
The goal is to read with “Can we really say that?”

## Skills You Build

- Mapping claim → evidence → conclusion
- Spotting weak “A therefore B” links
- Answering with counterarguments in mind
- Questions from a different stakeholder view (difficulty 5)

## Question Types

| Type | Content | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | Organize claim → evidence → conclusion | 3–5 |
| “Can we really say that?” | Point out insufficient logical links | All |
| Conditions for the conclusion | What must be true for the conclusion to hold | 1–5 |
| Counterargument response | Respond logically to a likely objection | 2–5 |
| Different stakeholder | Questions that depend on the reader’s role | 5 (business only) |

## Material and Format

| Difficulty | Material | Format | Questions |
|---|---|---|:---:|
| 1 (Intro) | Chat, short email | Three short, separate passages | 3 |
| 2 (Basic) | Progress report, minutes | Three short, separate passages | 3 |
| 3 (Standard) | Proposal / plan | One document | 4 |
| 4 (Advanced) | Analysis / approval document | One document | 4 |
| 5 (Expert) | Strategy / recommendation | One document | 5 |

Difficulty 1–2: independent short texts. Difficulty 3–5: one document, multiple questions.

## Tips

For “Can we really say that?”, ask: Could B still hold without A? Is a condition missing?

For “conditions for the conclusion”, think about unstated premises the author assumes.
