# Build-Up Tab

This tab trains you to read incomplete or ambiguous business situations, chain hypotheses, and reach a sound judgment.
An AI manager asks follow-up questions to surface gaps in your reasoning, then you deliver a final decision.

## Skills You Build

- Separating certain facts from uncertain information
- Building judgment step by step through linked hypotheses
- Updating your hypothesis when new information arrives
- Articulating a final decision with clear logic

## Flow

1. **Read the situation** — quantitative data mixed with statements and context
2. **Answer each STEP** — sort facts, then state your current judgment
3. **Manager follow-up** — one focused question on the weakest part of your reasoning
4. **Additional information** (difficulty 3+) — new facts that may challenge your hypothesis
5. **Final judgment** — logic mode or delivery mode

## Final Answer Modes

| Mode | What to write |
|---|---|
| Logic | Concise conclusion and reasoning; delivery style is not graded |
| Delivery | A message addressed to a specific person; how you communicate is graded |

## Difficulty Overview

| Level | STEP rounds | Extra info | Counterargument prompts |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1 | 1 | None | None |
| 2 | 1–2 | None | None |
| 3 | 2 | Once | None |
| 4 | 2–3 | Once | Yes |
| 5 | 3 | Twice | Yes |

## Tips

In STEP A, label **certain facts**, **uncertain information**, and **contradictions** explicitly — it improves later judgments.

Treat manager follow-ups as hints for your next move, not as failure. If no follow-up appears, your answer was sufficient at that stage.
