# Consideration Tab

You read a situation and write a message that works for the reader.
The AI plays the reader, reacts, and you revise through dialogue.
The goal is to **anticipate what the reader needs to know or do next**.

## Skills You Build

- Designing text for the reader’s position
- Structuring information for the purpose
- Avoiding casual or wordy phrasing; writing concisely
- Reviewing your own draft objectively

## Flow

1. **Read the situation** — Setting, reader, and information to convey
2. **Write** — Check the situation and work instruction (when, who, what, where); write so the reader can act or decide without confusion
3. **Reader reaction** — The AI asks clarifying questions
4. **Revise** — Improve using what the reaction revealed
5. **Grading** — Multi-axis feedback on the final message

## Scene Types

| Theme (scene) | Examples |
|---|---|
| Report & share | Progress, results, minutes |
| Request & instruct | Tasks, specs, procedures |
| Propose & explain | Plans, problem framing, concepts |
| Self-expression | Introduction, profile, PR |

## Difficulty

| Difficulty | Reader | Rounds | Situation |
|---|---|:---:|---|
| 1 (Intro) | One person, simple relationship | 1 | Clear what to convey |
| 2 (Basic) | One person, light stakes | 1 | Some easy-to-omit detail |
| 3 (Standard) | Two readers, different roles | 2 | Different info per reader |
| 4 (Advanced) | 2–3 readers, conflicting interests | 2 | Must support a decision |
| 5 (Expert) | Several readers, emotional care | 3 | Complex; priority of what to say |

## Grading Axes

- **Rounds to clarity** — Fewer rounds to zero open questions scores higher
- **Clarity as writing** — Can the reader act or decide without guessing?
- **Structure & readability** — Right granularity, consistent labels and format

## Checklist When Writing

- Is the next step clear for the reader?
- Any missing information?
- Bullets or numbers for structure?
- Avoid casual fillers (“kind of”, “you know”, etc.)
- Same level of detail in the same format?
- Avoid nested, hard-to-parse phrases?
