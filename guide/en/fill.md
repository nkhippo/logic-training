# Fill-in Tab

In business documents (email, reports, proposals, etc.), some linking expressions are blank.
You choose the correct word from context. The goal is to understand **why that word is logically required**, not just to guess.

## Skills You Build

- Reading how sentences connect
- Telling similar words apart
- Seeing the whole flow of a passage (Fully Supplemented Text)

## Document Types by Difficulty

| Difficulty | Document type | Blanks | Hints |
|---|---|:---:|:---:|
| 1 (Intro) | Internal email, operational notices | 2 | Yes |
| 2 (Basic) | Minutes, progress reports | 3 | Light hints |
| 3 (Standard) | Excerpt from proposal / plan | 3 | No |
| 4 (Advanced) | Analysis / survey report | 4 | No |
| 5 (Expert) | Strategy / consulting document | 5 | No |

## How to Read Feedback

After grading, "Fully Supplemented Text" is displayed.
It fills in every link between sentences so you can check the full flow, not only the blanks.

For each question you get:
- One everyday usage example
- How the argument would change if you used a different linker in this passage

## Tips

Before choosing a word, ask how the previous and next sentences relate:
continuation, contrast, or cause-and-effect. That makes the right linker easier to pick.
